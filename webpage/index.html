Research Project

Global Events vs. Whale Wallets

An exploratory data analysis examining whether macro-level global events or the coordinated activity of large cryptocurrency holders ("whales") more strongly drives price volatility and market behavior.

Research Question: What impacts the crypto market more — global events or whale wallets?

📋 Project Introduction

Cryptocurrency markets are uniquely volatile, shaped by forces that range from geopolitical shocks and macroeconomic policy to the strategic movements of a small number of extremely large holders. This project investigates which category of influence — broad global events or concentrated whale wallet activity — has a more measurable and consistent impact on crypto market behavior.

To explore this question, we collected historical price data, on-chain transaction records, and timestamped global event data spanning [DATE RANGE]. We define "global events" as major economic, political, or public-health events with documented market-wide effects, and "whale wallets" as blockchain addresses holding more than [X] BTC/ETH. The five visualizations below each illuminate a different facet of this relationship, culminating in a comparative impact analysis.

🗄️ Data Description

The dataset used in this project combines three primary sources. First, historical OHLCV (Open, High, Low, Close, Volume) price data for Bitcoin (BTC) and Ethereum (ETH) was sourced from the CoinGecko API, covering [DATE RANGE] at daily granularity — approximately [N] rows and [M] attributes including price, volume, and market cap.

Second, on-chain whale transaction data was obtained from Etherscan / Whale Alert API, capturing wallet addresses with holdings above a defined threshold, timestamped transaction events, and transaction value in USD. This dataset contains approximately [N] records across [M] attributes.

Third, a manually curated event log of [N] major global events (e.g., COVID-19 declaration, Fed rate announcements, exchange collapses, regulatory rulings) was assembled from news archives and annotated with date and estimated market sentiment. All three datasets were joined on date for the comparative analyses below.
